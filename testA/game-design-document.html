🎮 Multiplayer Horror Narrative

Game Design & Technical Requirements Document

1. Project Overview

A lightweight, browser-based multiplayer horror game focusing on a deep, stressful narrative with light puzzle elements. The aesthetic is low-poly/voxel (similar to Roblox/Minecraft) with a grid-based inventory system.

Feature Specification
Player Count 1 to 50 players per lobby. Empty slots are filled by scripted NPCs ("Fake AI").
Perspective First-person or Third-person (developer's choice for optimization).
Aesthetic Low-poly/Voxel (Roblox/Minecraft style)
Inventory Grid-based system with equippable bags

2. Technical Stack & Architecture

Frontend HTML5, CSS, lightweight Vanilla JS
(or Phaser/Three.js for 3D)
Networking (Primary) Peer.js (WebRTC)
P2P Host/Client architecture
Networking (Fallback) Node.js + WebSockets
Socket.io for lobbies >10 players
Optimization Chunk loading, distance fog
Low-poly assets for high FPS

⚠️ Critical Architecture Note:

Peer.js (WebRTC) will crash or heavily lag with 50 P2P connections. Node.js server must act as the authoritative server for lobbies >10 players to prevent lag and desync.

Physics & Vehicles:

Use local coordinate spaces for vehicle interiors so players do not clip or fly away when the vehicle moves.

3. Core Gameplay Mechanics

🎭 Roles System

Players cycle through three distinct roles across chapters:

Main Survivor Researcher Police

Each role has unique uniforms, items, and spawn locations.

🖱️ Interaction System

  • Right-click: Interact, pick up items, or sit in vehicle seats
  • Vehicle Entry: Players become children of vehicle's local coordinate space

🎒 Inventory System

  • Grid-based system (Minecraft/Roblox style)
  • Equippable bags for extra storage
  • Drag-and-drop item management

🤖 NPC System

  • Scripted "Fake AI" NPCs populate the world
  • Approximately 10 active NPCs per scene
  • Maintains illusion of crowded environment

💀 Death Mechanic (Chapter 2 Scene 2):

High lethality scene implemented. Dead players enter "Spectator Mode" or become Ghost NPCs for the remainder of the chapter.

4. Storyboard & Level Design

📖 Chapter 1: The Survivors

Role: Main Character

Scene 1: Gutenberg Departure

  • Players spawn in a fenced 4-story house boundary
  • Board one of 3 detailed, boxy buses (decorated with stars/holes)
  • Trigger: Bus departs when all seats are filled or countdown timer ends
  • Unseated players are teleported to random seats

Scene 2: The Endless Road

  • Nighttime, horror atmosphere with distant spaceship in the sky
  • Mechanic: One player drives (controls bus within large bounding box)
  • Road/trees scroll to create "infinite" illusion
  • Threats: Roadside shooters and incoming rocks
  • Bus takes damage; eventually, unavoidable massive rock crashes the bus

Scene 3: Jungle Crash Site

  • Massive jungle environment (uses chunk loading/fog)
  • Broken buses scattered throughout
  • Ghosts patrol but cannot enter bus wrecks
  • Objective: Build campfire → Scavenge iron → Craft antenna → Establish police signal

📖 Chapter 2: The Investigation

Roles: Researcher & Police

Scene 1: Researcher Prep

  • Players are Researchers; NPCs are Officers
  • Objective: Talk to lead Officer, pack gear in mini-lab truck compartment
  • 10-second timer to board 5 trucks and depart

Scene 2: The Haunted House

  • Arrive at massive, terrifying house
  • Objective: Conduct ghost research
  • High lethality scene (most players will die)
  • Survivors board the truck to escape

Scene 3: Police HQ

  • Players are now Police officers in large HQ with NPC cops
  • Objective: Receive briefing about missing persons in the jungle
  • Board police vehicles (cars/buses)

Scene 4: Return to Gutenberg

  • Police arrive at Chapter 1 starting area
  • Interrogate NPCs
  • Depart in vehicles to transition to Chapter 3

📖 Chapter 3: The Climax

Main Character Police

Scene 1: The Spaceship (Survivors)

  • Survivors navigate the jungle with firearms
  • Follow green smoke to massive, broken spaceship
  • Trigger: Once all enter, doors lock and ship automatically launches into space

Scene 2: Jungle Escape (Police)

  • Police arrive at jungle crash site
  • Ambushed by ghosts
  • Objective: Scavenge supplies and return to police vehicles
  • Strict 3-minute survival/escape timer

Scene 3: Ship Defense (Survivors)

  • Inside the moving spaceship
  • Aliens breach the hull
  • Objective: Survive and defend ship's interior for set duration
  • Ship reaches destination after defense period

Scene 4: Capture (Ending)

  • Ship docks at massive Alien Mothership
  • Players are overpowered, arrested, and locked in cell
  • Final Shot: Looking out window at Earth in the distance
  • End of Season 1

5. Developer Fixes & Missing Elements Addressed

1. 🔧 Networking Limit Fix

Problem: Peer.js (WebRTC) crashes or heavily lags with 50 P2P connections.

Solution: Mandate Node.js server for lobbies >10 players. P2P only for small groups (≤10 players).

2. 🚌 Vehicle Physics Fix

Problem: Moving buses cause players to fly/clip through geometry.

Solution: Implement local coordinate space parenting for interior players. Players become children of vehicle transform.

3. 💀 Death Mechanic (Previously Missing)

Problem: Chapter 2 Scene 2 has high lethality with no death handling.

Solution: Implement "Spectator Mode" or allow dead players to become Ghost NPCs for remainder of chapter.

4. ⏱️ Pacing Fix

Problem: Original 1-minute timers in Chapter 3 too short for "long story" horror experience.

Solution: Adjusted to 3-minute timers to allow for proper tension building and gameplay.

5. 🎬 Scene 3/4 Overlap Fixed

Problem: Original prompt had confusing overlapping space/police scenes in Chapter 3.

Solution: Separated clearly into:

  • Scene 3: Survivor Space Defense (inside spaceship)
  • Scene 4: Final Capture (Alien Mothership docking)
